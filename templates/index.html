Live Mouse Control

Gesture Based Live Mouse Control

An innovative way to control your mouse through simple hand gestures.

Project Overview

This project uses computer vision and machine learning to detect hand gestures that control mouse movements on the screen. It employs technologies such as OpenCV, Mediapipe, and PyAutoGUI to achieve this real-time interaction.

Key Features

Mouse Movement

Control mouse cursor with smooth hand gestures.

Left Click

Perform left-click using thumb and index finger gestures.

Right Click

Trigger right-click actions with specific hand movements.

Zoom In

Zoom in on the screen with specific hand gestures.

Zoom Out

Zoom out on the screen with specific hand gestures.

Screenshot

Capture the screen with a simple hand gesture.

Applications:

The potential applications for this project are vast and can be extended to a variety of domains, including:

  • Accessibility

    This system can be used to provide a hands-free computing experience for people with disabilities or limited mobility.

  • Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR)

    Gesture-based control is an essential component of VR and AR applications, providing users with a more immersive and natural interaction experience.

  • Gaming

    Hand gestures could be mapped to specific actions in games, allowing for a more immersive and interactive gaming experience.

  • Presentation Control

    Gesture-based control can be used during presentations, allowing presenters to control slides, navigate documents, and interact with digital content without needing physical input devices.

  • Medical and Research

    Gesture-based interaction can be used in research or medical fields, such as in robotic surgery or controlling machines in sterile environments where touch is not ideal.
